                  <td class="projectdesc">
                    Accessibility &amp; Game Adoption &nbsp; &#9670; &nbsp;
                    <em>Research</em>
                    <hr />
                    <p>
                      Project Advisors:
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">James Fogarty</a>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      My primary research explores the current strategies gamers with disabilities use during the game
                      adoption process, to advise game developers, publishers, and distributors on how they can better
                      support these practices. This work seeks to address research questions across the three main
                      phases of the "game adoption process":
                    </p>
                    <ul>
                      <li>
                        <b>Game Discovery.</b> What resources currently exist to support gamers with disabilities in
                        discovering games to play, and how can we support access needs in this interaction?
                      </li>
                      <li>
                        <b>Game Evaluation.</b> How are gamers with disabilities supported (or not supported) in
                        evaluating whether a game meets their access needs, and how can game publishers and distributors
                        better accommodate players' existing evaluation methods?
                      </li>
                      <li>
                        <b>Game Adaptation.</b> How can we support gamers with disabilities in adapting inaccessible
                        games to meet their access needs? What strategies are currently used, and how can these
                        strategies inform the design of tools to make the adaptation process easier?
                      </li>
                    </ul>

                    <p>
                      A11yhood: Supporting PWD in Adopting Access Technologies &nbsp; &#9670; &nbsp; <em>Research</em>
                    </p>
                    <hr />
                    <p>
                      Project Collaborators: Tony Fast,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">James Fogarty</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Jen Mankoff</a>, Joshua Miele, Ed Summers
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      There are more access technologies (ATs) available than ever before, but current systems do not
                      enable people with disabilities to effectively discover the ATs that will best suit their access
                      needs. Additionally, PWD invest tremendous energy in customizing and adapting existing
                      technologies to suit their specific needs and preferences, but that work is seldom available for
                      others to readily use. This project seeks to design a system--
                      <i><a href="a11yhood.org">A11yhood</a></i
                      >-- to enable easier discovery, adaptation, and collaboration for open-source AT and AT
                      modifications.
                    </p>

                    <p>
                      Models of Accessibility &nbsp; &#9670; &nbsp;
                      <em>Research</em>
                    </p>
                    <hr />
                    <p>
                      Project Collaborators:
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Kelly Avery Mack</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Emma McDonnell</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Aaleyah Lewis</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">James Fogarty</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Jen Mankoff</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Cynthia Bennett</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Leah Findlater</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Heather D. Evans</a>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      "Accessibility" doesn't have a universal definition: depending on the individual and the context,
                      what makes an interaction "accessible" can vary wildly. So naturally, the question becomes: what
                      do we actually mean when we talk about accessibility? This collaboration seeks to identify factors
                      that affect a contextual definition of accessibility, including individuals' unique intersectional
                      perspectives, goals and values in interaction, physical and social context, and more.
                    </p>

                  <td class="projectdesc">
                    <p>Ability Heuristics for Technology Evaluation &nbsp; &#9670; &nbsp; <em>Research</em></p>
                    <hr />
                    <p>
                      Project Collaborators:
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Claire Mitchell</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Judy Kong</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Jacob Wobbrock</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Alexis Hiniker</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Amy J. Ko</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Shaun Kane</a>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      Accessibility research has produced ample best practices for designing technology accessibly, but
                      few resources exist to meaningfully characterize <i>how</i> accessible a given technology is. This
                      project aims to develop a set of concrete heuristics for technologists to evaluate the
                      accessibility of existing technologies and inform users and designers of their limitations/how
                      they can be improved.
                    </p>
                    <p><i>In submission to CHI 2026.</i></p>

                  <td class="projectdesc">
                    Accessible Tabletop Game Modification &nbsp; &#9672; &nbsp;
                    <em>Research &amp; Consulting</em>
                    <hr />
                    <p>
                      This research focuses on identifying access barriers in tabletop and board games and developing
                      modifications to make the games more accessible. These modifications can take a wide range of
                      approaches including physical manipulation of game assets, development of digital tools, and
                      design of alternative game modes and gameplay styles.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      As part of this work, I have also had the opportunity to professionally consult for
                      <em>Exploding Kittens</em> on several of their games, identifying access barriers and designing
                      workarounds and game variants to improve the games' accessibility.
                    </p>

                  <td class="projectdesc">
                    Accessible Data Visualization Experiences &nbsp; &#9670; &nbsp; <em>Research</em>
                    <hr />
                    <p>
                      Project Collaborators:
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">John Thompson</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Bongshin Lee</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Alper Sarikaya</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Ed Cutrell</a>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      In this project, we aim to design and develop a system that enables Blind & Low Vision Screen
                      Reader Users to meaningfully interact with web-based data visualizations using an off-the-shelf
                      screen reader, with a particular emphasis on exposing the communicative intent and functionality
                      of a visualization. To design such a tool, we use co-design methods with a team of BLV
                      individuals, and iteratively design, prototype, & evaluate our tool in collaboration with our
                      design partners.
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      This project is a collaboration with the MSR EPIC & Ability teams, and was part of my 2022 summer
                      internship work.
                    </p>

                  <td class="projectdesc">
                    Repairing Android Game Inaccessibility &nbsp; &#9670; &nbsp;
                    <em>Research</em>
                    <hr />
                    <p>
                      Project Advisors:
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">James Fogarty</a>,
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Jake Wobbrock</a>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      Project Collaborators:
                      <a class="people" target="_blank">Mingyuan Zhong</a>
                    </p>
                    <p>
                      This project involves identifying, characterizing, and repairing accessibility barriers in Android
                      games. This work seeks to address many different research questions, including:
                    </p>
                    <ul>
                      <li>
                        How can game developers &amp; publishers better support gamers with disabilities in evaluating
                        the accessibility of games?
                      </li>
                      <li>What is the current state of accessibility in the space of Android educational games?</li>
                      <li>
                        How can we design and build accessible, dynamically-generated interfaces to help users overcome
                        accessibility barriers?
                      </li>
                    </ul>
